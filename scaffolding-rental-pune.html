CIVIL ENGINEERING GUIDE

Material Planning & Safety Guidelines

Essential considerations for planning material loading, vertical support distribution, and scaffolding alignments at your construction site.

1

Slab Load & Support Calculation

Always support heavy wet concrete with adequate vertical compression prop jacks. Our telescopic jacks should be spaced dynamically based on the thickness of the concrete slab (generally 1 prop per 10-12 square feet for a standard 5-inch house slab). Adjust screw collar threads for level horizontal layouts before pouring.

2

Cuplock Scaffolding Alignment

For multi-tier structures, ensure Cuplock vertical standards run strictly plumb. Fit horizontal ledgers into the top/bottom cups at every 0.5m to 1.5m vertical span depending on height load factors. Always deploy screw base jacks on solid ground foundations to prevent soil settlement accidents.

3

Pre-Pour Civil Inspection Checklist

Before casting slabs, civil engineers should carefully verify: 1) Alignment plumbness of H-frames and cup joints, 2) Complete security of pin-locks, swivel couplers, and wedge assemblies, and 3) Laying of anti-skid MS walk-boards / challi with secure tie attachments to prevent loose shifting under manual worker movement.
